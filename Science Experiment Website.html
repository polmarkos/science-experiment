Experiment 1: Baking Soda and Vinegar Volcano

Materials:

  • Baking soda
  • Vinegar
  • Container (such as a small bottle or cup)
  • Food coloring (optional)

Procedure:

  1. Place the container on a tray to catch spills.
  2. Add 2-3 tablespoons of baking soda into the container.
  3. Pour in vinegar slowly and watch the eruption happen!
  4. If desired, add food coloring to the vinegar for a colorful effect.

Expected Results:

The mixture of baking soda and vinegar will react to produce carbon dioxide gas, creating a fizzing eruption that looks like a volcano!

Experiment 2: Simple Paper Airplane Flight Test

Materials:

  • Paper
  • Ruler (optional, for measuring distance)
  • Timer (optional, for timing flight duration)

Procedure:

  1. Take a sheet of paper and fold it in half lengthwise.
  2. Unfold and fold each edge to the center crease to form the wings.
  3. Test the plane's flight by throwing it and measuring how far or how long it flies.
  4. Try adjusting the design of the plane and test again to improve its flight.

Expected Results:

The plane should glide through the air, and you can experiment with different designs to see which one flies the farthest or stays in the air the longest!
